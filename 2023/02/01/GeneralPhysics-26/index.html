Field, and Electric Field

A field is something that can be defined anywhere in space. Fields represent physical quantities.

  • scalar fields like T(x,y)T(x,y)T(x,y)
  • vector fields like V⃗(x,y,z;t)\vec{V}(x,y,z;t)V(x,y,z;t) and gravitation fieldg⃗\vec{g}g​

Physical fields, considered continuous and well-behaved, obey a simple rule and are created by sources. The rule and the sources decide the field.

gravitation fieldg⃗\vec{g}g​ is measured using a test body of small mass m0m_0m0​( does not disturb the mass distribution!). Similarly the electric field is measured by a small test charge q0q_0q0​.
