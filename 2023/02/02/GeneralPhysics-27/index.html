Flux Φ\PhiΦ

Definition

A single loop: A single loop of area AAA is placed in the stream, whose plane is perpendicular to the direction of the flow. Flux of the loop is the fluid volume passed through the loop per unit time.

Flux of Electric Field/ Electric Flux

ΦE=∑E⃗⋅A⃗,ΦE=∯E⃗⋅dA⃗\Phi_E = \sum \vec{E}\cdot\vec{A},\quad \Phi_E = \oiint\vec{E}\cdot{\rm d}\vec{A} ΦE​=∑E⋅A,ΦE​=∬​E⋅dA

The flux of electric field is the number of lines of electric field passing through the closed surface.

Specially, the integral over a closed surface is Electric Flux.

Note

  • Electric Flux is a scalar quantity.
  • dA⃗{\rm d}\vec{A}dA is normal to the surface and points out of the surface. Sign matters a lot!
  • Physical meaning: Electric Flux is the sum of the normal components of the electric field all over the surface.
  • For an open surface, the direction of A⃗\vec{A}A could be determined in two different ways.

Gauss’ Law

∯E⃗⋅dA⃗=ΦE=qenclosedϵ0\oiint \vec{E}\cdot{\rm d}\vec{A} = \Phi_E = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\epsilon_0} ∬​E⋅dA=ΦE​=ϵ0​qenclosed​​

The net electric flux through any closed surface is proportional to the charge enclosed by that surface.

    E⃗⋅dA⃗=EdA,ifE⃗∥dA⃗\vec{E}\cdot{\rm d}\vec{A} = E{\rm d}A, \quad if \quad \vec{E}∥{\rm d}\vec{A} E⋅dA=EdA,ifE∥dA
